The Sea of Sri Lanka

Turtle hatchery

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Center, which aims to preserve some of the world's endangered turtle species, is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town. The southern shore of the island is home to a number of turtle hatcheries, including this one.

Sri Lankan turtle hatcheries are more important for several reasons:

Conservation of endangered species: Sri Lanka is home to several species of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Loggerhead, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. Turtle hatcheries play a crucial role in protecting and conserving these species by providing a safe environment for eggs to hatch and releasing the hatchlings into the sea.

Research and education: Turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka also contribute to scientific research and education.

Ecotourism and economic benefits: Sri Lankan turtle hatcheries have become popular tourist attractions, drawing visitors from around the world.

In summary, Sri Lankan turtle hatcheries are essential for the conservation of endangered sea turtles, protecting them from poaching, conducting research, educating the public, promoting ecotourism, and maintaining the ecological balance of marine ecosystems.
